The 5-Breath Star

Hold up one hand. Trace a finger up and down each finger. Breathe IN as you trace up, and breathe OUT as you trace down. Do all 5 fingers. Feel calmer?

My Feeling Drawing

Get some crayons or markers. What color is your feeling? Draw it! Scribble hard if you're mad, or use bright colors if you're happy. There's no wrong way to draw a feeling.

Wiggle & Shake

Stand up and shake your whole body! Wiggle your arms, shake your legs, and jump around for one minute. Shaking out the "sillys" or "mads" feels great!

Be a Buddy, Not a Bully

Bullying Hurts Others...

When someone is bullied, it makes them feel sad, scared, and alone. It's like putting a rain cloud over their head that's hard to get rid of. Everyone deserves to feel safe and happy at school.

...But It Hurts YOU, Too!

This is the tricky part. Being unkind might seem powerful for a second, but it actually hurts you, too.

  • You lose friends. It's hard for kids to trust or want to play with someone who is unkind.
  • You get in trouble. Teachers and parents will find out. This can mean losing recess or fun time.
  • It builds a bad habit. Being kind is a skill, just like soccer or reading. If you practice being mean, you get good at being mean. This makes it much harder to make friends or be on a team as you get older.
  • It feels yucky inside. Making someone else feel bad usually ends up making you feel bad, too, like a heavy feeling in your tummy.

What Can You Do?

Be the person who includes others. If you see someone alone, ask them to play. If you're feeling mad, try one of the activities. If you see someone being unkind, tell a grown-up you trust.

Feeling Happy!

Awesome! When you feel happy, it's like having sunshine inside you. Here are some fun ways to share that sunshine:

  • Tell someone a silly joke.
  • Draw a picture for a friend or family member.
  • Sing your favorite song... LOUD!
  • Do a "happy dance."
  • Give someone a high-five!
üò¢

Feeling Sad

It's okay to feel sad. It's like a rain cloud visiting for a little while. Here are things you can do:

  • Talk to a grown-up you trust.
  • Hug a favorite teddy bear or pillow.
  • Draw or write about your feeling.
  • Listen to some calm music.
üò°

Feeling Mad

Whoa, a volcano! Feeling mad is a strong feeling. It's okay to be mad, but it's not okay to hurt. Try this:

  • Stomp your feet hard on the ground.
  • Punch a pillow (never a person!).
  • Do the 5-Breath Star activity.
  • Rip up some old paper or junk mail.
  • Ask for some alone time to cool down.
üò®

Feeling Scared

Feeling scared is your body telling you to be careful. It's okay! Here's what can help:

  • Tell a grown-up right away.
  • Turn on a light if it's dark.
  • Hold a favorite blanket or toy tight.
  • Think of a superhero who is brave.
  • Take a few slow, deep breaths.
ü§™

Feeling Silly

You've got the giggles! Being silly is super fun. You can:

  • Make funny faces in a mirror.
  • Tell a silly joke or make a funny noise.
  • Do a wiggly-jiggly dance.
  • Make sure it's a good time for everyone (not during quiet time!).
üòä

Feeling Shy

Feeling a little quiet? That's called being shy, and it is perfectly okay.

  • It's okay to just watch and listen.
  • You can give a small wave or a smile.
  • You don't have to talk until you feel ready.
  • Find one friend to talk to quietly.

About The Feelings Clubhouse

Welcome! The Feelings Clubhouse was created as a friendly, safe place for kids (and their grown-ups!) to learn about big emotions.

Our mission is to provide fun activities and simple guides to help understand that all feelings are okay, and to show how being kind to others is the best way to be.

This site is for informational and educational purposes. We hope it helps start positive conversations!
